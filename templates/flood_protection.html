Flood Preparedness

10 Tips to Protect Yourself and Your Neighbours

Floods. It’s just a little water, right? People don’t tend to take them too seriously, unlike other natural disasters like earthquakes. Yet, floods have the capacity to cause significant damage too. They displace people from their homes, cause large scale property damage, and even loss of lives. Floodwater can take days or even weeks to recede, prolonging the disruption to our lives. If you live in an area at high risk of flooding, here are some tips to minimise the impact of flooding.

1. Plan Your Evacuation Route

Work out the safest route from your home or work to the nearest evacuation centres. Identify any potential movements that could be cut off by floodwaters. In case you do not make it to your evacuation centre, take note of any high points along the way to wait for rescue safely.

2. Check for warnings and updates

Regularly check our website for flood warnings and updates, especially during periods of heavy rain. Tune in to the local radio station as well for similar instructions and updates.

3. Make an emergency kit

Your emergency kit should be within easy reach and ready to go. It should contain 3 days’ worth of drinking water and long-life food, toiletries; like soap, toilet paper, sanitary items, toothpaste and toothbrush, first aid kit, waterproof bags for valuables; including cash and documents, protective blankets and clothing, sunscreen and insect repellent.

4. Inform family, friends, neighbours, and vice versa

Ensure those close to you know what your plans are, in case of separation during a flood.

5. Don’t walk or drive through floodwater

Floodwater may hide any uneven terrain, which could potentially cause injuries and drowning. Floodwater also causes aquaplaning and skidding, increasing risk of accidents.

6. Wear solid shoes and bring along a long stick

If you have to walk through floodwater, ensure you have proper footwear, as the ground may be slippery, and use a stick to check the depth and current of the water.

7. Stay off bridges over fast-moving water

Bridges can be easily swept away without warning by fast-moving water.

8. Stay away from fallen power lines

Electricity can be conducted through water, so stay far away to avoid electrocution.

9. Don’t turn on electricity or gas before checking with professionals

Similarly, there is a risk of electrocution if you turn on electricity and appliances before it is safe to do so. Gas supply and appliances may be flooded and unsafe to use, and attempting to do so before checking may cause fires or explosions.

10. Boil tap water

Lastly, water supply may be contaminated after flood events. Remember to always boil tap water before consumption
